Kentucky Fried Chicken Recipe

The recipe was originally created by Colonel Sanders, who later own the largest fried chicken company, KFC. His images are the symbols of the company and well recognized.

His recipe is confidential, but it seems to be leaked all over the internet. In this article, I'm going to introduce the most popular one.

To make this fried chicken, you will need:

  • Chicken. It can be chicken breasts, thighs, drumsticks, ... It's all personal reference.
  • Butermilk
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 2 teaspoons onion salt
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • Half teaspoon celery salt
  • Half teaspoon dried sage
  • Half teaspoon garlic powder
  • Half teaspoon ground allspice
  • Half teaspoon dried oregano
  • Half teaspoon dried basil
  • Half teaspoon dried marjoram
  • 1 and half cups Gold Medal™ all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1 egg white
  • And the most important, frying oil

That is a lot of ingredients (yes it is), but it's worth it.

Here are the steps to make this iconic recipe:

  1. Preheat fryer to 350℉, and mix all the herbs and spices,
  2. Combine spices with flour, brown suger and salt.
  3. Dip chicken pieces in egg white to lightly coat them, then transfer to flour mixture. Turn a few times and make sure the flour mix is really stuck to the chicken.
  4. Let chicken pieces rest for 5 minutes so crust has a chance to dry a bit. Remember don't rest it for too long.
  5. Fry them in fryer. It should take about 12-14 minutes depend on chicken sizes. Chicken pieces are done when a meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part reads 165℉.
  6. Let chicken drain oil and enjoy with ketchup or chili sauce.

This is one of my favourite recipe because it's easy to make (though spices preparation might seem complex), and most important, it's delicious.
